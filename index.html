Hi, I’m Yuer Tang. I’m majoring in Data Theory, with a self-designed curriculum aimed at exploring computational cognitive science. My core interest lies in the statistical modeling of the computational basis of human cognition.

Specifically, I’m focused on understanding how humans can learn so rapidly from minimal data. I approach this from a statistical inference perspective, studying how we make inductive leaps—generating many ideas from just a few experiences. My goal is to apply these principles to both better understand human cognition and to develop more powerful machine learning algorithms.
